Chapter 4. Product description

bitcontrol® Digital TV Link is an input filter and part of bitcontrol® Multimedia Suite for Microsoft® Windows® .

The filter runs as a built-in DirectShow® component in Windows Media® player, in bitcontrol® multimedia products as well as in conjunction with other multimedia players, as long as they are able to load DirectShow® filters.

The bitcontrol® Digital TV Link receives the video stream from a digital source, fragments it like a demultiplexer and forwards the parts to other processing components.

The video signal can be provided by ethernet, USB or from an external video source (e.g. STB, encoder, TV stick) or can come from a PC internal source (e.g. PCI DVB card, video file).

Figure 4.1. Scheme of bcDTVLink


The multimedia player creates a filter graph from the filters and codecs, which are available in Microsoft® Windows®. On the input side there is the video source, which is divided into its audio and video parts by bitcontrol® Digital TV Link (working as a demultiplexer or as a splitter, respectively). These two streams are then decoded by the video and audio decoder. Subsequently these signals are transferred to and output in the respective output filter (also called renderer), optionally via a mixer.

To display the filter graph, the Microsoft® program GraphEdit of the DirectX® SDK can be used. See also the Figure 4.2, “bitcontrol® Digital TV Link filter graph”.

Figure 4.2. bitcontrol® Digital TV Link filter graph


The bitcontrol® Digital TV Link is very robust against input failures such as drop outs of the input stream, bit errors and other signal noise making it very suitable for DVB-T, DVB-S and internet streaming applications.

The bitcontrol® Digital TV Link receives the packetised elementary video streams (PES) from a Set Top Box or from another PC running the bitcontrol® Digital TV Link in multicast mode.
